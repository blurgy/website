Nouns

Latin

Every Latin noun has a

  • gender (masculine, feminine, or neuter),
  • number (singular or plural), and
  • case (nominative, genitive, dative, accusative, ablative, vocative, or locative).
The gender determines the gender of any adjectives that may modify the noun. The number denotes the quantity of the noun. The case is determined by the function of the noun in the sentence.

There are five declensions of Latin nouns. To decline a noun, one removes the ending from the genitive singular form to obtain the noun's stem, to which the following endings are appended:

Declension I

Most nouns of the first declension are feminine, some are masculine, and none are neuter.
