Latin Pronouns

Personal Pronouns | Reflexive Pronouns

A pronoun is a word used instead of a noun.

Personal Pronouns

A personal pronoun represents the speaker (I, we), addressee (you), or a third person spoken about (he, she, it, they). Personal pronouns have different forms depending on whether the noun is first- second- or third-person in relation to the speaker.

First Person - 'I, we'

Second Person - 'You'

The genitive forms of the first- and second-person personal pronouns can be used as Partitive or Objective Genitives only. In the plural these functions of the genitive are distinguished by different endings: -um = partitive, -i_ = objective.

Third Person - 'He, they'

Third Person - 'She, they'

Third Person - 'It, they'

The third person personal pronouns are in origin demonstrative adjectives (this, that, these, those), and retain this alternate usage.

Reflexive Pronouns

When a pronoun refers to the subject of the sentence, it is called reflexive.

The declensions for the first- and second-person reflexive pronouns are identical to those for the personal pronouns, but without the nominative case. As with personal pronouns, the genitive forms can be used as Partitive or Objective Genitives only.
